### [1] [Is Trend Still Your Friend?: A Microstructural Account of the Demise of Short-Term Trend-Following](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01550)
*Jutta G. Kurth,Zoltan Eisler,Adam Rej,Jean-Philippe Bouchaud*

Main category: q-fin.TR

TL;DR: 系统化趋势跟踪策略在2009年后短期趋势失效。通过对1995-2025年约100个流动性期货合约的分析，发现波动率归一化的最小价格变动单位（tick size）是区分趋势策略表现优劣的关键变量：小tick合约上趋势收益崩溃，大tick合约上仍保持有效。三个候选解释（容量约束、市场电子化、CTA与订单流交互的制度变化）均不成立。

Motivation: 系统性趋势跟踪策略长期盈利，但2009年后短期趋势不再提供可靠收益。作者试图解释这一结构性断裂的原因，并区分不同合约间的表现差异。

Method: 使用1995-2025年约100个流动性期货合约的截面数据，结合行业代表性CTA代理指标，按信号速度、资产类别分类分析。检验四个候选解释，核心发现基于波动率归一化tick size的横截面比较。

Result: 小tick合约上趋势收益崩溃，大tick合约上趋势收益保持完整。资产类别和流动性无法复现这一二分法。容量约束、电子化、CTA与订单流交互变化三个解释在时机、规模和横截面异质性上失败。

Conclusion: 趋势异常的核心是自实现反馈循环：趋势信号驱动方向性交易，市场冲击强化价格变动。后危机时代高频做市商的流动性撤回行为对小tick（稀疏）和大tick（密集）限价订单簿影响迥异，在小tick合约上打破了这个循环，而大tick合约上残余深度仍足以维持该机制。

Abstract: Systematic trend following has, on average, been profitable for at least two centuries; yet since approximately 2009, short-term trends have ceased to deliver reliable returns. Using a cross-section of roughly 100 liquid futures contracts spanning 1995-2025, together with an industry-representative CTA proxy, we document the break and characterise its dependence on signal speed and asset class. We evaluate four candidate explanations - capacity constraints, market electronification, a regime change in CTA-versus-order-flow interactions, and a microstructural mechanism - and find that the first three fail on grounds of timing, magnitude, or cross-sectional heterogeneity.
  Our central empirical finding is that the cross-sectional variable distinguishing degraded from surviving trends is the volatility-normalised tick size: post-2008 trend PnL has collapsed on small-tick contracts across all signal horizons, while remaining essentially intact on large-tick ones. Neither asset class nor liquidity replicates this dichotomy.
  We interpret this result through a self-fulfilling feedback loop that, in our view, lies at the heart of the trend anomaly itself: trend signals trigger directional trades, whose market impact reinforces the very price moves that generated the signal. Both the profitability and the persistence of trend are sustained by this impact channel, which requires that trend followers can execute aggressively at reasonable cost. We argue that the post-crisis transition to HFT-dominated market making, whose liquidity-withdrawal behaviour in front of predictable directional flow has sharply contrasting consequences for sparse (small-tick) and dense (large-tick) limit order books, has broken this loop on small-tick contracts. On large-tick contracts, residual depth remains sufficient, and the loop continues to operate.
